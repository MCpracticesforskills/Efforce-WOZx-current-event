by: Morgan Coyle

WOZX Is Here and It's a Big Deal for Energy Efficiency Projects World Wide

The next item on the world’s political agenda is energy efficiency. We have to save the environment. We’ve seen politicians like Al Gore repeat this mantra to ad nauseam (Gore made numerous environmental predictions that were horribly wrong but let’s not go down that road). Project Veritas even caught CNN Technical Director Charlie Chester saying CNN will be using propaganda to push climate change awareness (again, let me stop myself here.) Whether you believe the climate is in dire straits or not, we can all agree the environment needs our help.

With the global population growing at an exponential rate, it is vital that both public and private industry do their part to save the environment. That’s where Efforce comes in. Efforce is a company that provides a platform. Their platform lists energy efficiency projects for investors and businesses who are looking to fund energy efficiency projects. These projects increase a company’s efficiency while saving the environment. The amount of energy saved (aka megaWoz just like megawatts except with a cleaver name) is then recorded using smart meters. The more energy a company saves, the more megaWoz they generate. The megaWoz is then distributed back to the company and the investors in WOZX cryptocurrency.

Still not comprehending? Don’t worry. Efforce is the first company to attempt using Blockchain technology, cryptocurrency, and energy efficiency in this unique way. Let me give a solid example. Company A needs to re-lamp their warehouse and switch from gas to nuclear power, but Company A doesn’t have enough money to do it on their own. So, Company A lists their project (re-lamping and nuclear power needs) with Efforce. Investors see the project. Investors invest in project. Project is completed. Company A gets new lamps in their warehouse; they function off of nuclear power. The day is saved. Now, all the energy Company A saves is documented on the Blockchain, and each megawatt is converted into WOZX. WOZX is then distributed back to Company A and the investors.
